    Clam Soup with Basil and Ginger

    Background Story

    Since the weather doesn’t get too cold in Taiwan or California, I prefer my soups to be on the lighter side than on the heavier, creamy side. However, the clam soup my parents and grandparents make is perfect for any weather, whether it be hot or cold outside. The soup is not too filling and can be consumed prior to the start or at the end of the meal. The freshness of the clams can be appreciated in the broth and the flavors from the basil and ginger complement that from the clams very well. If you like seafood or would love to start, or end, your main meal with a wonderful palate cleanser, this soup is for you!

    Ingredients Needed

    • Clams
    • Basil
    • Ginger
    • Rice Wine

    Method

    1. To properly prepare the ingredients, rinse the basil and ginger. Slice the ginger up into thin slivers. Ensure that the clams are well-cleaned by placing it in saltwater for at least 30 minutes
    2. Heat up a pot of water on high heat. Once the water boils, add the clams into the pot.
    3. Cook the clams until the shell is slightly ajar, then turn the heat down to small. Add in a bit of rice wine to remove potential shellfish aftertaste, along with ginger. For taste, add in a pinch of pepper and salt.
    4. After 1-2 minutes or until the clams are fully open, turn off the heat. Add the basil and serve!

    Tip

    The heat should be turned off after the clams are fully open or else the mean will lose its tenderness. In addition, the ginger taste will mask all flavors in the soup if it is heated for too long.

  • Shrimp Fried Rice

    Shrimp Fried Rice

    Background Story

    I spent most of my life near the ocean, so it is given that seafood is one of my favorite foods, shrimp being one of them. The tenderness of the shrimp, combined with the flavor from the rice, is something that I crave whenever I am away from home. Fortunately, this dish is quite easy to make so home is not too far away! With this recipe, I hope you’ll enjoy this dish

    Ingredients Needed

    • Rice (3 cups)
    • Shrimp (1/2 cup)
    • Green Onion (2 stalks)
    • Garlic (2 cloves)
    • Egg (1)
    • Soy Sauce (1 tablespoon)
    • Salt and Pepper for tasting

    Method

    1. Prepare the ingredients: ensure that the shrimp is peeled and deveined, the green onion and garlic are properly cut and diced.
    2. Add some oil to the pan on medium heat. Add in the egg and stir quickly.
    3. Add in the garlic and shrimp and stir until the aroma is noticeable
    4. Add the rice to the pan and flip and stir to ensure that the grains are separated from one another
    5. Add soy sauce, green onions, salt, and pepper to the pan and continue to stir. After 1-2 minutes, the fried rice should be ready for serving.

    Tip

    To ensure that the grains of rice are not stuck together when stir-fried on the pan, place the bowls of rice in the fridge for at least 4-6 hours so that the individual grains can harden.

  • Konbu Broth

    Konbu Broth

    Background Story

    My family enjoys clear, lighter broth over cream-based ones and when we make hot pot, konbu broth is an amazing base that has all the aspects we look for. Konbu has a slight salty yet sweet taste to it and taste good even on its own! Konbu broth can be prepared by refrigerating it or heating it up! Attached below is a simple way as to how konbu broth can be prepared with cold water! Enjoy!

    Ingredients Needed

    • Konbu Seaweed (14g)
    • Shiitake Mushrooms (6g)
    • Water (1 Liter)

    Method

    1. Soak the mushrooms and konbu in cold water for 2-3 hours. The broth should be ready for drinking or use after.

    Tip

    The hotter the room temperature, the longer konbu needs to be soaked in cold water. Konbu can also be soaked in water in the fridge as well.

  • Stir-Fried Yam Leaves

    Stir-Fried Yam Leaves

    Background Story

    My fondest memories of my time in Taiwan include dining out at seafood restaurants located near where I lived. Though my family and I would order dishes upon dishes of seafood for all of us to share, they would usually order some vegetables to go alongside it, yam leaves being one of them. These leaves taste good regardless of whether they’re seasoned or not and contain plenty of nutrients such as zinc, vitamin B, and iron. Attached below is a recipe on how you can stir fry some yummy yam leaves! Enjoy!

    Ingredients Needed

    • Yam Leaves (200g)
    • Garlic (5 cloves)
    • Sliced Ginger (10g)
    • Garlic (2 cloves)
    • Rice Wine (1 teaspoon)
    • Soy Sauce (1 tablespoon)
    • Salt and Pepper (1 pinch each)

    Method

    1. On high heat, add oil and fry the ginger and garlic until it is golden brown
    2. Add the yam leaves into a pan and cover it up. Let it cook for around 1-2 minutes.
    3. Remove the lid, add salt and pepper to taste. Quickly stir for a minute or so and the dish is ready for serving

    Tip

    For the yam leaves to have a slight crisp to it, the pan needs to be heated at a high enough temperature.

  • Shrimp Rice Rolls

    Shrimp Rice Roll

    Background Story

    It’s a bummer that there aren’t many (or any, for what it’s worth) places to get dim sum in Pittsburgh but shrimp rice rolls are my go-to order whenever I’m eating dim-sum back at home. The softness of the rice rolls combined with the tender shrimp is one to crave for. The sauce adds the needed sweet and salty flavor to the rice roll and does it just right – not too sweet, not too salty. While quarantined at home last year, I learned that this dish is not too complicated to make. Regardless of where you are, whether it be in California where there are a lot of dim-sum places nearby or Pittsburgh where there aren’t, this dish is one that enables you to bring the dim-sum flavors to your home.

    Ingredients Needed

    • Rice Flour (150g)
    • Shrimp (1/2 cup)
    • Potato Starch (75g)
    • Water (600g + 3 tablespoons)
    • Egg (1)
    • Soy Sauce (3 tablespoons)
    • Salt and Pepper for tasting
    • Sugar (1 tablespoon)

    Method

    1. In a bowl, add the rice flour, potato starch, and water (600g) and mix until the consistency is smooth
    2. Pat the shrimp with a paper towel until dry and mix the shrimp, egg, a pinch of salt and pepper each into another bowl
    3. Bring half a pot of water to boil
    4. Place the steaming pan onto the pot of water and add 1/8 of the flour and starch mixture. Ensure that the mixture evenly covers the pan
    5. Place 2-3 shrimp on top of the mixture
    6. Cover the steaming pan and on high heat, steam for three minutes
    7. Cool the pan for 30 seconds and roll the rice roll up.
    8. Mix the soy sauce, sugar, and water (3 tablespoons) together to create the sauce. Add the sauce onto the shrimp rice roll and enjoy

    Tip

    The rice roll may be stuck to the steaming pan at first so to prevent the roll from being stuck too much to the pan, oil can be brushed onto the steaming pan before the mixture is added. That way, the roll can be removed from the pan in an easier fashion.

  • Miso Salmon

    Miso Salmon

    Background Story

    My family loves salmon for the nutrients it provides and that it can be prepared it many ways. Growing up, my parents after work would make this it and I looked forward to each night this was served. The umami, saltiness, and slight tang complemented with vegetables and rice that was served alongside it and during college, I, at times, prepare this dish since it doesn’t take too long to prepare! I hope you enjoy this recipe!

    Ingredients Needed

    • White Miso (3 tablespoons)
    • Sake (2 tablespoons)
    • 6 oz salmon filets (4)
    • Soy Sauce (2 tablespoon)
    • Sugar (2 tablespoons)

    Method

    1. Prepare the marinate by adding miso, sake, sugar, and soy together in a bowl. Mix well.
    2. Marinate the salmon filets for at least 30 minutes
    3. Broil the salmon filets for 6-10 minutes. Broil times may vary based on how hot the broiler is or how thick the filets are.

    Tip

    Marinate the salmon for at least 4 hours if you want the salmon to have a stronger taste
